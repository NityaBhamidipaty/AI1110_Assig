\begin{enumerate}
\item[2 (c)] Jaya borrowed \rupee50,000 for 2 years. The rates of interest for two successive years are 12\% and 15\% respectively. She repays \rupee33,000 at the end of the first year. Find the amount she must pay at the end of the second year to clear her debt.\\
    \solution \\
For the $1^{\text{st}}$ year, \\
Principal amount: $P = \text{\rupee50,000}$\\
Rate of interest: $R = 12\%$ p.a \\
Amount due at the end of $1^{\text{st}}$ year 
\begin{align*}
    &= P\left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)
    \\
    &= 50000\left(1 + \frac{12}{100}\right)
    \\
    &= 50000\times \frac{112}{100}
    \\
    &= \text{\rupee56,000}
\end{align*}
Amount paid at the end of $1^{\text{st}}$ year = \rupee33,000\\
$\implies$ Principal for the $2^{\text{nd}}$ year
\begin{align*}
    &= \text{\rupee56,000} - \text{\rupee33,000}
    \\
    &= \text{\rupee23,000}
\end{align*}
For the $2^{\text{nd}}$ year,\\
Principal amount: $P =$ \rupee23,000\\
Rate of interest: $R = 15\%$ p.a \\
Amount to be paid at the end of $2^{\text{nd}}$ year
\begin{align*}
    &= P\left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)
    \\
    &= 23000\left(1 + \frac{15}{100}\right)
    \\
    &= 23000\times \frac{115}{100}
    \\
    &= \text{\rupee26,450}
\end{align*}
\end{enumerate}
Hence, Jaya must pay \rupee26,450 at the end of second year to clear her debt.
